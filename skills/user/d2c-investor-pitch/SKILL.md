### Pitch Deck — Slide by Slide

| Slide # | Title | What It Must PROVE | Common Mistake | What Great Looks Like |
|---------|-------|-------------------|----------------|----------------------|
| 1 | Cover | This is a credible brand | Cluttered logo slide | Clean, one-line positioning |
| 2 | The Problem | This pain is real and large | Generic "people want X" | Specific data point or story |
| 3 | The Solution | Your product uniquely solves it | Product description dump | The "aha moment" in one visual |
| 4 | Market Size | TAM/SAM/SOM | Copying Statista numbers blindly | Bottom-up calculation |
| 5 | Traction | Something real is happening | Vanity metrics | Orders, repeat rate, margins |
| 6 | Product | What it is and why it wins | Too many features | The one thing that makes you different |
| 7 | Business Model | How money flows | Complex revenue streams | Simple: price, margin, channel |
| 8 | Go-to-Market | How you'll grow | "We'll do social media" | Specific channel with evidence |
| 9 | Competition | You know the landscape | Ignoring competitors | Honest positioning map |
| 10 | Team | Why you'll win | Listing credentials | Why this team for this problem |
| 11 | Financials | The numbers make sense | 100x projections | Conservative base case with logic |
| 12 | The Ask | What you need and why | Vague "looking for partners" | Specific: ₹X for Y milestone in Z months |

### Pre-Pitch Checklist
- [ ] Deck is under 12 slides
- [ ] Every slide has ONE clear point
- [ ] Traction is the most prominent metric
- [ ] Ask is specific (₹ amount + use of funds)
- [ ] You can say the 60-second pitch without looking at slides
- [ ] You know your gross margin, CAC, and LTV by heart
- [ ] You have a data room ready if they ask for one
